# **THE CHAKHO DIVISION (착호대 · Chakho-dae)**

*"The Tiger Suppression Corps"*
—but in your world, the “tiger” is a metaphor for **rogue or powerful spirits**.

This name keeps the **historical resonance** of 착호갑사, while transforming it into a militarized, mechanized, secretive spirit-hunting force.

---

# 1. **Public Cover Identity**

Officially, the Chakho Division is:

> **“A rural safety corps tasked with controlling dangerous beasts and protecting travel routes.”**

Most commoners think they fight:

* man-eating animals
* mountain beasts
* bandits

In reality, they are the government’s **primary spirit-hunting and fragment-retrieval force**.

---

# 2. **True Purpose (Secret Mandate)**

### **“Suppress, extract, deliver.”**

They locate and eliminate:

* rogue spirits
* wandering Ki remnants
* fragment-rich spirits with high industrial value
* spirits resisting conscription or extraction
* fragments improperly harvested by civilians

They work under direct authority of the Iron King.

The public never sees the bodies.
Only rumors.

---

# 3. **Organizational Structure**

## **1. The Five Claws (오조 · Ojo)**

The leadership council, each “claw” representing a different specialty.
Each is named after an aspect of the mythical tiger.

### **1) The White Claw (백조)**

*Operations & dispatch*

* assigns squads
* marks spirits as Class-Rogue or Class-Fragment-Prime

### **2) The Black Claw (흑조)**

*Stealth & elimination*

* assassination of dangerous spirits
* covert missions
* nighttime retrievals

### **3) The Crimson Claw (적조)**

*Close-combat and containment*

* experts in Ki-binding nets
* physical suppression
* shock troops for large spirits

### **4) The Iron Claw (철조)**

*Engineering & machinery*

* designs Ki-siphoning devices
* enhances weaponry (bows, rifles, steamgear)
* maintains spirit cages

### **5) The Silent Claw (묵조)**

*Internal affairs / purges / interrogations*

* investigates traitors
* enforces secrecy
* “disappears” anyone who questions the work

The Silent Claw is the most feared part of the organization.
This is where the threat to your protagonist would come from once his hybrid nature is suspected.

---

## **2. Field Units (갑사대 · Gapsa-dae)**

These are hunters trained similarly to the original 착호갑사 but infused with Ki-tech and steam weaponry.

There are three main types:

### **A. Grey Tigers (회호)** — Trackers

* Ki-sense training
* long-distance pursuit
* spirit pattern reading
* archer specialists (your protagonist fits here originally)

### **B. Bronze Tigers (동호)** — Suppressors

* heavy infantry
* Ki-dampening gauntlets
* physical restraint teams
* steam-armor users

### **C. Hollow Tigers (공호)** — Fragment Collectors

* carry extraction tools
* trained in clean harvesting
* almost emotionless, ritualistic
* terrifying to other divisions

---

## **3. The “Tiger Scrolls” (호문 · Homun)**

A secret archive listing:

* every recorded spirit
* intent patterns
* fragment value
* methods to kill or disable each type
* forbidden research

Almost no one is allowed to read these.
The Iron King keeps the originals, but the Chakho Division guards the copies.

---

# ✔ 1. **“Each is named after an aspect of the mythical tiger” — What does that mean?**

Historically and culturally, the **tiger (호랑이)** in Korean folklore represents:

* protection
* ferocity
* vigilance
* mountain spirits
* guardianship
* punishment of evil

A *mythical* or *spirit* tiger (산신호랑이) has symbolic “aspects” or metaphysical qualities.
So each “Claw” (the 5 leadership branches) is named after one symbolic attribute of the folklore tiger.

Here are more grounded examples:

---

## **The Five Tiger Aspects → The Five Claws (오조)**

### **1. White Tiger (백호 / Baekho)** — Direction & Judgment

The White Tiger is a celestial guardian associated with the west, metal, autumn, clarity.
→ So **White Claw** handles
**classification, dispatch, assessment.**
*They judge what threats must be hunted.*

---

### **2. Black Tiger (흑호 / Heukho)** — Shadows & Stealth

In folk tales, black tigers appear at night, protect graves, or deliver warnings.
→ So **Black Claw** handles
**stealth, assassinations, covert suppression.**

---

### **3. Red Tiger (적호 / Jeokho)** — Rage & Battle

This represents the tiger’s ferocity and fiery Ki.
→ So **Crimson Claw** handles
**combat squads, containment, shock troops.**

---

### **4. Iron Tiger (철호 / Cheolho)** — Endurance & Strength

An invention for your steampunk twist: a tiger of metal or enduring Ki.
→ So **Iron Claw** handles
**engineering, Ki-tech, weaponry, spirit-cages.**

---

### **5. Silent Tiger (묵호 / Mukho)** — Mysticism & Secrets

Folklore speaks of tigers that appear in dreams, omens, or in total stillness.
→ So **Silent Claw** handles
**internal affairs, purges, secrecy, interrogations.**

---

So “each is named after an aspect of the mythical tiger” simply means:

> **Each leadership branch reflects a symbolic quality associated with tigers in Korean myth — strength, stealth, judgment, endurance, mystery — and its name + function matches that meaning.**

No supernatural tiger needed — just symbolism informing the organizational language.

# ✔ 2. **“Class-Rogue” and “Class-Fragment-Prime” — What do these classifications mean?**

These were just placeholder category names — let’s turn them into something clearer, culturally grounded, and system-consistent.

## **Spirit Classification System Used by White Claw**

### **Class 1 — Wild (야영 / Yayeong)**

Unintelligent, weak spirits formed from minor Ki clusters (e.g., farm tools, lonely objects, wind sprites).
Handled by junior squads.

---

### **Class 2 — Rogue (탈령 / Tallyeong)**

Spirits that:

* manifest aggressively
* disrupt human settlements
* or resist peaceful dispersal

These are treated as threats and must be subdued or eliminated.

---

### **Class 3 — Guardian (수호령 / Suhoryeong)**

Large region-based spirits (mountain spirits, ancient tree spirits, river spirits).
Hunting them is **politically dangerous** and often suppressed from public knowledge.

---

### **Class 4 — Fragment-Prime (원정령 / Wonjeong-ryeong)**

Spirits with **high-density Ki cores** ideal for industrial extraction.
Very rare.
Often targeted secretly by order of the Iron King.

This class includes:

* battlefield memory spirits
* spirits formed from mass tragedies
* ancient or mythologically significant spirits

Your protagonist’s parent (the battlefield memory spirit) fits here, making him a walking Form-Prime anomaly.

---

### **Class 5 — Forbidden (금령 / Geumryeong)**

Spirits the King has ordered NOT to be killed publicly but may be secretly harvested by Chakho Division.
Sometimes includes:

* politically symbolic spirits
* spirits bound to ancestral rites
* spirits tied to national myths

The river guardian once belonged here — then its classification was quietly altered to Fragment-Prime shortly before its death.

# ✔ 3. **Who do the Field Units (Gapsa-dae, 갑사대) work under?**

They operate **directly under the Five Claws**, but each unit is assigned to one specific branch depending on their specialty.

Here is the chain:

---

# **Chain of Command Overview**

## **Iron King**

↓

## **High Overseer of the Chakho Division (호감 · Hogam)**

The King’s direct representative.
Only one person holds this position.

↓

## **The Five Claws Leadership (오조)**

Each “Claw” is a commander over a strategic division.

↓

## **Operational Commanders (부령 · Buryoung)**

These are sub-commanders under each Claw who:

* oversee missions
* deploy units
* train recruits

↓

## **Gapsa-dae Field Units (갑사대)**

These are the “tiger soldiers,” equivalent to the historical 착호갑사 but adapted for spirit-hunting.

They are divided into:

### **Grey Tigers — 회호**

Under **White or Black Claw** depending on mission.
Trackers, archers, scouts.

(Your protagonist works under **White Claw** initially — intelligence & assessment.)

---

### **Bronze Tigers — 동호**

Under **Crimson Claw**.
Heavy infantry, containment, shock troops.

---

### **Hollow Tigers — 공호**

Under **Iron Claw** or sometimes **Silent Claw**.
Fragment harvesters and handlers of spirit remains.

---

## **Special Ops (독립호 · Dokrim-ho)**

Rare, elite operatives who report only to **Silent Claw**.
Used for purges and traitor retrieval.

This is who gets sent after your protagonist once his hybrid nature is suspected.
